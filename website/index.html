ICEland

Your personal command-line tool for focused digital areas.

What is ICEland?

ICEland is a Rust-based command-line tool that helps you separate your digital life into clear, focused areas such as work, learning, math, gaming, traveling, and trading. Instead of mixing everything on one computer, ICEland creates structured environments so you can focus better and stay organized.

Who is this for?

  • Students who want to separate study, work, and personal time.
  • Developers who like clean, structured workflows.
  • Anyone who gets distracted by mixed files, notes, and browser activity.

What does ICEland do?

With one command, ICEland sets up:

  • Dedicated folders for each area.
  • Area-specific tools (notes, analysis, flashcards, etc.).
  • Separate browser profile spaces per area.
  • Useful links per area (Primuss, Wikipedia, games, Forex Factory, news, etc.).
  • A way to switch between areas instantly.
  • A way to reset (“destroy”) browser data or notes per area.

How to use it

Download the ICEland Linux binary from the project’s GitHub releases or build it yourself with cargo build --release. Then run it from your terminal:

Example Commands

iceland init
iceland switch learning
iceland notes learning "Study Rust at 5pm"
iceland destroy gaming browser
iceland status

How ICEland helps you focus

When you switch to learning, you see Primuss, Wikipedia, and ChatGPT links. When you switch to gaming, you see simple games. When you switch to trading, you see Forex Factory. This keeps your mindset aligned with your current task.

Future & Business Idea

ICEland is currently free and open source. In the future, a “Pro” version could include:

  • Automatic browser launching with real profiles.
  • Cloud backup of notes.
  • Better UI or GUI.
